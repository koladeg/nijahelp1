Hepatitis E

Definition

Hepatitis E is a liver infection caused by the hepatitis E virus (HEV) which is transmitted via the faecal-oral route.
Since the development of jaundice (yellow discoloration of the skin and sclera of the eyes, dark urine and pale stools)) is a characteristic feature of liver disease,
a correct diagnosis can only be made by testing patients' sera. In general, hepatitis E is a self-limiting viral infection followed by recovery.
Prolonged viraemia or faecal shedding are unusual and chronic infection does not occur.

Symptoms

The symptoms of hepatitis E range from subclinical to severe and act from three to eight weeks after infection
  1. Jaundice
  2. Anorexia (loss of appetite)
  3. An enlarged, tender liver (hepatomegaly)
  4. Abdominal pain and tenderness
  5. Nausea and vomiting.
  6. Fever

Prevention

  1. Drinking safe water
  2. Proper disposal of sanitary waste
  3. Detection of outbreaks, source of infection and mode of transmission

Treatment

HEV infections are usually self-limited, and hospitalization is generally not required.
However, hospitalization is required for severe hepatitis and should be considered for infected pregnant women.
As no specific therapy is capable of altering the course of acute hepatitis E infection,
prevention is the most effective approach against the disease.
