Heart Disease

Definition

Heart disease is becoming more and more common. Your heart pumps blood to your entire body through blood vessels.
It also has blood vessels inside the heart that feed it oxygen and nutrients.
The vessels in your heart can get clogged by fat, causing heart disease, stroke, or a heart attack.

Symptoms

Unfortunately, there usually are no signs of heart disease until the arteries (the blood vessels around the heart) are already blocked.,
Your doctor can test your heart to see how healthy it is. Schedule an appointment at least once a year.
You should go to the doctor right away if you have a dull or crushing pain in your chest, which you may get usually after some kind of physical activity

Prevention

  1. Exercise regularly.
  2. Quit or don't start smoking.
  3. Maintain a healthy weight
  4. Watch your blood pressure.
  5. Eat lots of vegetables, fruits, and whole grains
  6. Don't eat a lot of fatty foods
  7. Don't eat foods with too much cholesterol

Treatment

Emergency! You may be having a heart attack if you have:

  1. Severe chest pain
  2. Chest pain that travels to your arm, elbow, shoulder, back, or jaw
  3. A hard time breathing.
  4. Perspiration, a sick feeling to your stomach, vomiting, or dizziness
Take an aspirin or a painkiller if you have any of these problems and see a doctor
