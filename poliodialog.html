Polio

Definition

Poliomyelitis (polio) is a highly infectious viral disease, which mainly affects children under five year of age.
The virus is transmitted through contaminated food and water, and multiplies in the intestine, from where it can invade the nervous system.
Many infected people have no symptoms, but do excrete the virus in their faeces, hence transmitting infection to others.

Symptoms

  1. Fever
  2. Fatigue
  3. Headache
  4. Vomiting
  5. Stiffness in the neck
  6. Pain in the limbs

    7. In a small proportion of cases, the disease causes paralysis, which is often permanent.

Prevention

Polio vaccine, given multiple times, can protect a child for life

Treatment

There is no cure for polio, it can only be prevented
