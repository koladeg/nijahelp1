Ear infection

Definition

An ear infection (acute otitis media) is most often a bacterial or viral infection that affects the middle ear, the air-filled space behind the eardrum that contains the tiny vibrating bones of the ear.
Children are more likely than adults to get ear infections

Symptoms

  1. Earache is common but does not always occur.
  2. Dulled hearing may develop for a few days.
  3. fever is common.
  4. Children may feel sick or vomit and can be generally unwell.
  5. Young babies cannot point to their pain. One of the causes of a hot, irritable, crying baby is an ear infection
  6. Sometimes the eardrum bursts (perforates). This lets out infected mucus and the ear becomes runny for a few days. As the pain of earache is due to a tense eardrum, a burst eardrum often relieves the pain. A perforated eardrum usually heals within a few weeks after the infection clears.

Prevention

It is common for children to have two or more bouts of ear infection throughout childhood.
In most cases, there is nothing you can do to prevent the infection from occurring.
However, there is some evidence to suggest that an ear infection is less likely to develop:

  1. In breast-fed children.
  2. In children who live in a smoke-free home. Passive smoking of babies and children can increase the risk of developing ear infections, and various other problems.

Occasionally, some children have recurring bouts of ear infections close together. If this occurs, a specialist may advise a long course of antibiotics to prevent further bouts from occurring.

Treatment

Most bouts of ear infection will clear on their own without treatment within 2-3 days.
The immune system can usually clear germs (bacteria or viruses) that cause ear infections.
However, treatments that may be advised include the following:

  1. Painkillers
  2. Antibiotics - are prescribed in some cases only
