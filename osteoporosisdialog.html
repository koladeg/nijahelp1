Osteoporosis

Definition

Osteoporosis is when your bones get weak and break easily, even from bumping into a table or sneezing. Hip, spinal, and wrist fractures are the most common broken bones.
According to the National Osteoporosis Foundation of SA, one in three women get this disease.

Symptoms

  1. Back or neck pains.
  2. A hump in your back or a bent spine.
  3. Easily-broken bones, even if you don't fall or have injuries.
  4. Tenderness in your bones.

Prevention

    Women who don't get enough calcium and exercise when they're young can get the disease.
    It usually shows up after you turn 50 or when you have menopause. However, women as young as 25 do get the disease,
    so if you suspect anything, speak to your doctor or health care provider. Your bones start losing calcium when you turn 30,
    but you can keep them strong by eating well and exercising.

Treatment

Start a fitness routine when you're young. Develop healthy eating habits and make sure you get enough calcium.
If you can't eat foods with calcium in them, take vitamins and supplements. Talk to your doctor or health care provider to get more information
