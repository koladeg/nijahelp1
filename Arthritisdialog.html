Arthritis

Definition

The word "arthritis" means "joint inflammation." Inflammation is one of the body's natural reactions to disease or injury, and includes swelling, pain, and stiffness
Arthritis causes your joints to get very stiff and painful to move.
The most common types are osteoarthritis and rheumatoid arthritis. Osteoarthritis causes the destruction of your joints.
Rheumatoid arthritis affects your entire body and causes swelling or inflammation of your joints.
Arthritis usually doesn't show up until people reach their 40s

Symptoms

Osteoarthritis:
  1. Joint pain after exercise or physical activity.
  2. Stiff hands, knees, hips, or back after not moving around for a while.
  3. Swollen, tender joints.
  4. Joints making grinding noises or rubbing together when you move around
Rheumatoid Arthritis:
  1. Painful or stiff joints.
  2. Warm, red, and swollen joints.
  3. Fevers.
  4. Feeling tired or run-down.
  5. Lack of hunger.
